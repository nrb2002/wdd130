Rafting, a good remedy for PTSD patients

According to this article, Neurologists have found that patients with PTSD appear to have lower baseline cortisol levels and a reduced response to stress. Both adrenaline and cortisol are produced to help our bodies fight stress. Increasing them during voluntary exercise like rafting would therefore help our bodies prepare for unpredictable stressful situations.
